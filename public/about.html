The story

Why ClinicFlow exists

A few years ago, I was doing a summer research project on small business operations and spent a lot of time talking to dental clinic owners and office managers in the Montreal area. The problems they described were almost identical across every clinic I visited.

Missed calls went unanswered. Not because nobody cared — but because the front desk was already handling a patient at the desk, and by the time they called back, the person had already booked at the clinic down the street.

No-shows were a constant drain. Clinics knew reminders worked, but sending them manually was inconsistent. Patients slipped through.

Recall lists sat untouched. Every clinic had hundreds of patients due for a cleaning. Nobody had the time to work through the list consistently. Patients drifted away.

What made this strange was that software solutions existed — but they charged $200–$500 per month, required staff training, and often didn't fit the way a 2-chair clinic actually operates. The clinics I spoke with weren't looking for enterprise software. They wanted someone to just set it up and make it work.

So I built ClinicFlow: a done-for-you automation setup with a one-time price, no monthly fees, and no new software for the front desk to learn. I configure everything on top of tools the clinic already uses (Gmail, Mailchimp, their existing phone line), and the automations run quietly in the background.
