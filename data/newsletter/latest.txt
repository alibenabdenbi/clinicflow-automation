Something I noticed after the Victoria Day weekend.

We track callback patterns across 4,314 Canadian clinics. After long weekends, missed call rates spike — not during the weekend itself, but on the Tuesday morning after.

The pattern is consistent: patients who couldn't reach their clinic on Friday call again Tuesday. If they still can't get through, most don't try a third time.

The clinics that recover long-weekend call volume fastest are the ones that have some form of automatic acknowledgment in place — even something as simple as a text that says "we saw your call, we'll be in touch." Not a booking system. Just a signal that someone is there.

---

One data point from this week's research:

Clinics with patient communication complaints in their Google reviews average 0.26 stars lower than clinics without. In Toronto's market — where we've mapped 480 clinics — that gap means the difference between appearing in the top three Google Maps results and not appearing at all.

0.26 stars. One patient complaint about a missed call. Compounded over hundreds of reviews.

---

Genuine question for this week:

After a long weekend, do you know how many calls came in Friday afternoon and Tuesday morning that went unanswered? Not approximately — actually?

Most clinic owners I've spoken with estimate it's low. The call logs usually tell a different story.
